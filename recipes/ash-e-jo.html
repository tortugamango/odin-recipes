Ash-e-Jo (Persian Barley Soup)

Home

Description

Ash-e-Jo is a hearty Persian soup made with grains, legumes, fresh herbs, and a signature yogurt finish. Rich, creamy, and packed with nutrients, this comforting dish blends the earthy flavors of barley and lentils with fragrant herbs like dill and parsley. Finished with a turmeric-mint tarka and a dollop of yogurt, it's a soul-warming meal perfect for any season.

Ingredients

  • 60g dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 60g dried beans (e.g., kidney or white beans, soaked overnight)
  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 1/3 cup rice
  • 1/3 cup green lentils
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • Fresh dill (a handful, chopped)
  • Fresh coriander (a handful, chopped)
  • Fresh parsley (a handful, chopped)
  • Fresh spinach (a handful, chopped)
  • Olive oil (for sautéing)
  • Chicken broth (enough to cover, or as needed)
  • Yogurt (for serving)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper

Tarka (Spiced Oil)

  • 1 teaspoon dried mint
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced (optional for tarka)

Steps

  1. Make the stock: Prepare chicken broth using bones, onion, and garlic if making from scratch, or use pre-made broth.
  2. Fry the onion: In a large pot, heat olive oil and sauté chopped onions until golden brown.
  3. Combine ingredients: Add soaked chickpeas and beans, lentils, pearl barley, rice, and broth to the pot. Stir well.
  4. Pressure cook: Cook under pressure for about 20 minutes, or until everything is soft and tender.
  5. Add herbs: Stir in chopped dill, coriander, parsley, and spinach. Let simmer uncovered for another 10–15 minutes.
  6. Make the tarka: In a small pan, heat olive oil or ghee. Add turmeric, dried mint, and minced garlic (if using). Fry for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  7. Finish the dish: Pour the tarka over the soup. Stir gently to incorporate. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve: Spoon into bowls and top with a generous blob of yogurt. Serve hot with flatbread or as-is.
