Mung Bean Daal

Home

Description

This comforting mung bean daal is a warm, earthy, and mildly spiced lentil curry. Made with simple pantry ingredients like mung beans, garlic, tomato, and aromatic spices, it’s slow-simmered until creamy and finished with a flavorful tempering of ghee, garlic, and curry leaves. Perfect served with rice or chapati, it’s both wholesome and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mung beans
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 medium tomato, peeled and chopped
  • 3–4 green chilies
  • 6 curry or bay leaves
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste (about 3 cloves)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon salt (add at the end)

Tadka

  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 5–6 whole red chilies
  • 1/2 bunch thick chopped garlic
  • Thinly sliced onion (as preferred)
  • Curry leaves (a few sprigs)
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or butter

Steps

  1. Cook the daal: Combine mung beans, water, chopped tomato, green chilies, curry or bay leaves, chicken stock cube, garlic paste, red chili flakes, red chili powder, and turmeric in a pot.
  2. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for 30–40 minutes, or until the mung beans are tender and the daal is creamy. Stir occasionally.
  3. Add salt: Once the daal is fully cooked, add 1 teaspoon of salt and stir to combine.
  4. Prepare the tempering: In a separate pan, heat ghee or butter over medium heat. Add whole red chilies and chopped garlic. Sauté for about 2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Finish the tempering: Add curry leaves and cumin seeds. Let them sizzle until the garlic turns golden and aromatic.
  6. Combine: Pour the hot tempering over the cooked daal. Mix gently to blend the flavors.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice or fresh chapati. Enjoy!
