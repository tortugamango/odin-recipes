Peanut Stew

Home

Description

This rich and savory stew, popular in West African cuisine, is a beautiful blend of creamy peanut butter, tomatoes, spices, and vegetables. It's often made with sweet potatoes and greens, creating a perfect harmony of sweet, spicy, and nutty flavors. Whether you serve it with rice, couscous, or eat it on its own, it’s a soul-satisfying dish that’s both nutritious and deeply comforting.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1–2 teaspoons chili flakes or 1 fresh chili (optional, for heat)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne (optional)
  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 (14 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter (smooth or crunchy)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 4 cups chopped kale or spinach
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Roasted peanuts (for garnish, optional)

Steps

  1. Sauté aromatics: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for about 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Stir in garlic, ginger, and chili, cooking for another 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Spice it up: Add cumin, paprika, cinnamon, and cayenne. Stir to coat the onions and cook the spices for 1 minute.
  3. Add veggies: Toss in the sweet potato and bell pepper. Stir well, then add the canned tomatoes (with juices) and mix.
  4. Peanut butter magic: In a bowl, whisk together the peanut butter with a cup of broth until smooth. Pour this mixture into the pot along with the remaining broth.
  5. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and the stew has thickened.
  6. Add greens: Stir in kale or spinach and cook for another 5 minutes until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and lime juice to taste.
  7. Serve: Ladle into bowls and top with chopped cilantro and roasted peanuts if desired. Serve with warm rice, flatbread, or enjoy on its own.
